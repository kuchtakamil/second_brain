### 1. Slightly under the influence of alcohol  
*(subtle, often positive or neutral)*  
- **tipsy** – slightly drunk, “buzzed”  
- **a bit drunk** – somewhat drunk  
- **buzzed** – feeling alcohol, light euphoria  
- **merry** – cheerful from alcohol (slightly old-fashioned)  
- **had a drink / had a few drinks** – euphemistic, very mild  

---

### 2. Clearly drunk  
*(alcohol has a noticeable effect)*  
- **drunk** – neutral, most basic term  
- **intoxicated** – formal / legal  
- **under the influence** – formal, police/legal usage  
- **sloshed** – colloquial, British  
- **hammered** – slang, fairly strong  
- **buzzed / drunk** – depending on context  

---

### 3. Very drunk  
*(heavy intoxication, loss of control)*  
- **wasted** – very drunk  
- **hammered**  
- **smashed** – UK/US slang  
- **plastered** – slang  
- **trashed** – heavily drunk, often pejorative  
- **blitzed** – slang  
- **out of it** – incoherent due to alcohol  

---

### 4. Extremely drunk  
*(exaggerated, often humorous or cautionary)*  
- **blackout drunk** – drunk to the point of memory loss  
- **passed out** – unconscious  
- **falling-down drunk** – unable to walk  
- **three sheets to the wind** – idiomatic, very drunk  
- **dead drunk** – very colloquial  
- **blind drunk** – UK, extreme intoxication  

---

### Terms describing people (nouns)  
- **a drunk** – neutral, can be pejorative  
- **a heavy drinker** – someone who drinks a lot (not necessarily drunk at the moment)  
- **an alcoholic** – clinical / medical  
- **a boozer** – slang, UK  
- **a party animal** – euphemistic, lifestyle-related
