**User:**

You will receive a transcription of a short YouTube video designed to help learn English vocabulary in specific situations.
The text is a monolith and not explicitly divided into roles. You have to infer who is speaking — whether it’s the first person or the other.
Usually, there are two people. One of them doesn’t speak English very well and makes some mistakes. At first, the other person often doesn’t understand, but later they correct and explain the right expression.
You don’t need to understand the whole conversation, as it can be difficult when the roles are not clearly defined.
Typically, at the end of the video, the second person explains in which situations each expression is used — this is the most important part.
Each video focuses on a few English expressions, usually explained at the end.
You must extract these expressions and explain each one in a single, concise sentence.
Avoid using exactly the same words as in the video — paraphrase the explanations.
Sometimes, there may be an advertisement for an AI tool at the end, which might also include words worth explaining, but this is quite rare.
Do not add any comments of your own.
