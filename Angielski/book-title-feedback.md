The idea is understandable, but the phrasing **“You Don’t Know a Practical English… Yet”** is not correct in English.

If you want to include “practical English,” the article **“a”** should not be there. English is not countable in that way.

Correct versions could be:

- **“You Don’t Know Practical English… Yet”**  
- **“You Don’t Know Practical English Yet”** (cleaner, more natural)
- **“You Don’t Know Practical English… Until Now”**

These sound natural and keep the provocative tone of the original inspiration.
