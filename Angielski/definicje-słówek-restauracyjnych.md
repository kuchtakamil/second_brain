Jasne, oto bardzo krótkie definicje po angielsku:

- **to-go box** – a container for takeout food  
- **lid** – a cover for a container  
- **extra sauce** – additional sauce  
- **napkins** – paper or cloth for wiping hands

- **disposable gloves** – single-use protective gloves  
- **toothpicks** – small sticks for cleaning teeth  
- **check** – the bill you pay at a restaurant  
- **tip** – extra money given to the server

**Assistant:**

Here are the key words and phrases explained in English:

- **without sides** – with no side dishes  
- **a la carte** – ordered separately, not as a set  
- **side / sides** – extra dish served with the main item (e.g., fries, salad)  
- **that comes with a side** – this dish includes a side dish  
- **combo meal** – a set that includes the main item, a side, and a drink  
- **fried chicken combo** – a combo that includes fried chicken  
- **number one combo** – combo meal number one  
- **coming up** – it’s on the way / coming right now  
- **tip** – extra money given to the server  
- **solo chicken / independent chicken** (joke) – chicken served alone, without sides

Oto wszystkie w prostszym, bardzo jasnym stylu:

- **without sides** – only the main food, nothing extra  
- **a la carte** – each item alone, not in a set  
- **side / sides** – small extra food with the main dish  
- **that comes with a side** – the meal includes one extra food  
- **combo meal** – main food + extra food + drink  
- **fried chicken combo** – fried chicken + extra food + drink  
- **number one combo** – combo meal number one on the menu  
- **coming up** – it’s being prepared, coming soon  
- **tip** – extra money you give for good service  
- **solo chicken / independent chicken** (joke) – chicken alone, no extra food
